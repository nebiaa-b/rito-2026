Introduction

Prompt 1 – A Time I Made a Difference

My Written Response

Moving from Christchurch to Wellington meant starting again from scratch. I’ve always been quiet, so I naturally notice others who feel the same. Reaching out became my way of building connection.

Supporting My Friend:
I connected with Sana, an international student who was unsure and quiet. We studied together at our own pace, and her confidence grew — and so did mine.

Leadership in the Classroom:
As I helped Sana, other students joined us. The teacher trusted me to support the group while he worked with others. This wasn’t planned — leadership happened naturally.

Beyond the Classroom:
In Debate and Environment Club, I often checked on quieter students and paired people up. Inclusion became something I naturally practised.

Where It Led:
I was appointed Prefect for Pursuing Growth — not for being loud, but for being consistent, supportive, and genuine.

What I Want to Bring to Rito:
A leadership style rooted in noticing people, including them, and helping everyone feel like they belong.

Prompt 2 – The Biggest Barrier

My Written Response

Many young people feel Parliament is distant, intimidating, and “not for them.” They believe they need a perfect list of experiences to be taken seriously.

Why Experience Isn’t Everything:
Some students move cities or countries. Some restart their schooling. Some carry family or financial responsibilities. These students often have strong perspectives but fewer “official” leadership roles.

Friends like Sana and Tala are incredibly bright and thoughtful — the kind of people Parliament needs — but they hesitate because they don’t look like traditional candidates.

What We Should Value:
Experience matters, but so do perspective, potential, curiosity, and lived insight.

Why I Care:
I’ve moved cities myself. I know what it feels like to start from zero. I don’t have a long list of programmes — but I have commitment, curiosity, and a genuine interest in policy.

I want to be part of Rito not just to contribute, but to show that meaningful voices aren’t limited to perfect CVs.

Conclusion – Thank You

Engaging with Parliament shouldn’t feel like it’s only for the most confident or experienced. I’m applying because I’m ready to learn, contribute, and represent young people like me — the quiet ones, the new ones, the ones still finding their place.

I hope to bring steady leadership, genuine curiosity, and a commitment to including others. Thank you for considering my application. I'm excited about the chance to learn and contribute.

Note: The experiences I shared involving friends (such as Sana and Tala) are included with their full knowledge and consent.
